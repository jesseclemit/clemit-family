      Drug & supplement reference

      Quick-reference monographs in a familiar nursing-handbook format. Reference only — not a prescription

      Atorvastatin
      brand: Lipitor

      HMG-CoA reductase inhibitor (statin)
      Therapeutic useLowers LDL cholesterol; primary & secondary prevention of atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease.
      Usual adult dosing10–80 mg PO once daily, any time of day, with or without food. Titrate to LDL target / risk.
      Key considerationsAssess baseline lipids, LFTs, and CK if symptomatic. Counsel on muscle pain/weakness. Avoid large amounts of grapefruit juice. Pregnancy: contraindicated.
      Common effectsMyalgia, GI upset, headache. Rare: rhabdomyolysis, transaminase elevation, new-onset diabetes.
      MonitoringLipid panel 4–12 weeks after start/dose change, then periodically. LFTs as clinically indicated.
      Safety note Discontinue and evaluate promptly for unexplained muscle pain, tenderness, or weakness — especially with fever or malaise (possible myopathy/rhabdomyolysis).
      Vitamin D₃ (Cholecalciferol)
      OTC supplement

      Fat-soluble vitamin · supplement
      Therapeutic useCorrection/prevention of vitamin D deficiency; supports calcium absorption and bone health.
      Typical dosingMaintenance 600–2,000 IU/day for most adults; deficiency repletion per serum 25-OH-D level and clinician guidance. Take with a fat-containing meal.
      Key considerationsCheck serum 25-hydroxyvitamin D before high-dose repletion. Excess is stored — toxicity (hypercalcemia) is possible at sustained very high intakes.
      InteractionsThiazides (↑ hypercalcemia risk), corticosteroids (↓ effect), some weight-loss/cholesterol agents (↓ absorption).
      MonitoringSerum 25-OH-D; calcium if high-dose or symptomatic.
      Tirzepatide
      brands: Mounjaro, Zepbound

      GIP / GLP-1 receptor agonist
      Therapeutic useType 2 diabetes (Mounjaro) and chronic weight management (Zepbound); improves glycemic control and produces substantial weight loss.
      AdministrationOnce-weekly subcutaneous injection, same day each week, any time of day; rotate sites. Dose escalated gradually per labeling and tolerance.
      Key considerationsGI effects (nausea, diarrhea, constipation) common during titration. Protect lean mass with adequate protein and resistance training. Slower loss preserves muscle. Set goal weight by body composition, not scale alone, with the prescriber.
      CautionsPancreatitis history, severe GI disease, gallbladder disease; delays gastric emptying (affects other oral meds). Not for type 1 diabetes.
      MonitoringWeight, glycemic markers, GI tolerance; lean mass / body composition where available.
      Boxed warning (class) Thyroid C-cell tumors seen in rodents; contraindicated with personal/family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN 2. Confirm specifics with the prescriber.
